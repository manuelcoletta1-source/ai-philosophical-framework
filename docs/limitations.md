# Limitations

The AI Philosophical Framework is intentionally minimal.

It provides a structural model for representing operational events, but it does not attempt to capture every aspect of reality or decision processes.

This section clarifies the boundaries of the framework.

---

## Not a Complete Model of Reality

The framework does not attempt to describe the full complexity of real-world systems.

It focuses only on a minimal structure that allows events to be reconstructed through:

Decision  
Cost  
Trace  
Time

Other analytical layers may be built on top of this structure.

---

## Not a Legal Framework

The framework does not define legal responsibility or institutional authority.

It provides a structural representation of events that may support legal or governance analysis, but it does not replace legal systems.

---

## Not a Moral or Ethical System

The framework does not attempt to define ethical principles or moral evaluations.

It describes the operational structure of actions and consequences, leaving normative judgments to other disciplines.

---

## Limited Event Representation

Events represented within the framework are simplified descriptions.

They capture only the elements necessary to reconstruct an operational action.

Complex contextual factors may exist outside the framework.

---

## Dataset Limitations

The dataset included in the repository is small and experimental.

It is intended only as a demonstration of how events may be represented.

It should not be interpreted as a comprehensive dataset.

---

## Ontology Scope

The ontology included in the framework provides a minimal set of classes and properties.

It may be extended over time, but the current version intentionally avoids excessive complexity.

---

## Experimental Status

The framework is currently experimental.

Concepts, schemas, and datasets may evolve as research continues.

The goal is to explore whether a minimal representation of operational events can support analysis of complex decision environments.
