# Future Work

The AI Philosophical Framework is an experimental research project.

Future development may expand the framework in several directions while preserving the minimal operational structure.

---

## Ontology Expansion

The current ontology provides a minimal set of concepts and relationships.

Future work may include:

- additional agent classifications
- more detailed cost categories
- extended trace typologies
- richer event relationship models

These additions may improve analytical capabilities while maintaining structural simplicity.

---

## Larger Operational Datasets

The current dataset is intentionally small and illustrative.

Future development may include:

- larger collections of operational events
- datasets derived from real-world systems
- curated research datasets
- synthetic event datasets for experimentation

These resources may support research on decision analysis and AI auditing.

---

## AI Analysis Tools

The framework may eventually support software tools designed to analyze operational events.

Possible tools include:

- event validation systems
- decision chain reconstruction tools
- operational auditing pipelines
- AI-assisted event classification systems

These tools could make the framework easier to apply in practical environments.

---

## Knowledge Graph Systems

The RDF/OWL representation allows the framework to function as a knowledge graph.

Future work may explore:

- integration with graph databases
- semantic reasoning systems
- automated inference over decision chains
- large-scale event knowledge graphs

---

## Interoperability

Future versions may explore connections with existing standards and ontologies.

Possible areas include:

- governance data standards
- AI accountability frameworks
- semantic web ontologies
- distributed system monitoring standards

Interoperability may allow the framework to interact with broader ecosystems.

---

## Research Collaboration

As the framework evolves, collaboration with researchers and developers may expand its scope.

Future work may include:

- collaborative ontology development
- experimental datasets
- cross-disciplinary research projects
- open research initiatives

---

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term goal is to explore whether a minimal operational representation of decisions and consequences can support analysis of complex technological and institutional environments.
