# Frequently Asked Questions

This document answers common questions about the AI Philosophical Framework.

---

## What is the AI Philosophical Framework?

The AI Philosophical Framework is a minimal structure for representing operational events across human, institutional, and artificial systems.

The framework describes events through the sequence:

Decision · Cost · Trace · Time

This structure allows events to remain reconstructible and observable over time.

---

## Is this a philosophy project or a software project?

It is both.

The framework begins as a philosophical model of action and responsibility, but it is designed to become machine-readable through schemas and structured representations.

The goal is to create a bridge between conceptual analysis and computational systems.

---

## Does the framework replace legal or governance systems?

No.

The framework does not attempt to replace legal, ethical, or institutional systems.

Instead, it provides a minimal structure that can support the reconstruction and analysis of events within those systems.

---

## What kinds of agents can generate events?

Events may be generated by different types of agents:

- human individuals
- institutions
- artificial intelligence systems
- autonomous machines

All agents are evaluated using the same operational structure.

---

## Why are "Cost" and "Trace" important?

Many systems record actions but do not explicitly capture the consequences or observable traces produced by those actions.

The framework introduces cost and trace to ensure that events remain reconstructible.

---

## Can the framework represent complex processes?

Yes.

Complex processes can be represented as **decision chains**, where multiple operational events occur in sequence.

See:

docs/decision-chain.md

---

## Is the framework complete?

No.

The framework is intentionally minimal and experimental.

It is designed to evolve through research and practical experimentation.
