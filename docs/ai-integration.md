# AI Integration

The AI Philosophical Framework can be used by artificial intelligence systems to analyze and represent operational events.

The framework provides a minimal structure for describing actions and decisions through the sequence:

Decision · Cost · Trace · Time

AI systems can use this structure to evaluate whether an event is operationally reconstructible.

---

## AI Event Analysis

An AI system can analyze an event by identifying the four core elements.

Step 1 — Decision  
Identify the action selected by the agent.

Step 2 — Cost  
Identify the resources, risks, or irreversible consequences generated by the decision.

Step 3 — Trace  
Identify the observable record produced by the event.

Step 4 — Time  
Verify that the event is temporally exposed and reconstructible.

If all four elements are present, the event can be classified as an operational event.

---

## AI Decision Chains

AI systems often generate sequences of decisions.

The framework can be used to represent decision chains where each step produces:

Decision → Cost → Trace → Time

This allows AI systems to generate structured explanations of their actions.

---

## Explainability

The framework may support explainable AI systems by forcing decisions to be described through reconstructible elements.

Instead of abstract explanations, AI systems can report:

- what decision was made
- what cost was generated
- what trace remains
- how the event persists in time

---

## AI Auditing

AI auditing systems may use the framework to evaluate whether recorded events are operationally valid.

Events missing one or more elements of the sequence may be classified as incomplete or narrative.

---

## Possible Integrations

The framework may be integrated with:

- AI governance systems
- autonomous system logs
- distributed decision systems
- operational accountability tools

The goal is not to constrain AI behavior but to provide a minimal structure through which AI decisions can be reconstructed and analyzed.
