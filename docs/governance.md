# Framework Governance

The AI Philosophical Framework is an open research project.

Its development follows a structured but flexible governance model intended to preserve conceptual clarity while allowing experimentation and improvement.

---

## Maintainers

The framework is maintained by contributors who review and integrate changes to the repository.

Maintainers are responsible for:

- preserving the minimal operational structure of the framework
- reviewing contributions
- ensuring consistency across documentation, ontology, schemas, and datasets

---

## Contribution Process

Contributions are welcome through the standard GitHub workflow.

Typical steps include:

1. Open an issue describing the proposed change.
2. Discuss the change with maintainers and contributors.
3. Submit a pull request with the proposed modification.
4. Maintain a clear explanation of the change and its purpose.

---

## Types of Contributions

Possible contributions include:

- ontology extensions
- schema improvements
- additional example events
- dataset extensions
- documentation improvements
- research use cases

---

## Versioning

The framework uses versioned releases.

Version numbers reflect structural changes to the framework.

Examples:

v0.1 — initial research release  
future versions may introduce new ontological elements or schema refinements.

---

## Stability

The framework is currently experimental.

Structural components may evolve as research progresses.

However, the minimal operational structure is expected to remain stable.

---

## Long-Term Development

The framework may evolve into a broader ecosystem including:

- extended ontologies
- larger operational event datasets
- AI auditing tools
- decision chain analysis systems
- knowledge graph applications

The long-term goal is to explore whether a minimal representation of operational events can support analysis of complex decision environments.
