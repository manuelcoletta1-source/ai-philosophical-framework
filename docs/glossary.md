# Glossary

This glossary defines the core terms used in the AI Philosophical Framework.

The goal is to provide clear and minimal definitions that remain consistent across documentation, schemas, and examples.

---

## Operational Reality

Operational reality refers to events that can be reconstructed and verified through the sequence:

Decision · Cost · Trace · Time

Events that do not satisfy this sequence may exist as narrative or interpretation, but they cannot be operationally verified within the framework.

---

## Decision

A decision occurs when an agent selects one possibility among multiple alternatives.

Agents may include:

- humans
- institutions
- artificial intelligence systems

A decision marks the transition from potential action to actual action.

---

## Cost

Cost represents the resources, risks, or irreversible changes generated by a decision.

Examples include:

- energy consumption
- economic expenditure
- opportunity loss
- system risk
- environmental impact

Cost indicates that the decision has real consequences.

---

## Trace

A trace is the observable effect produced by an event.

Traces enable reconstruction of events.

Examples include:

- logs
- database records
- documents
- sensor data
- physical outcomes

Without trace, events cannot be reconstructed.

---

## Time

Time exposes events to verification.

Temporal exposure allows systems to observe persistence, consequences, and potential decay of events.

Time transforms isolated actions into reconstructible histories.

---

## Agent

An agent is the entity responsible for initiating a decision.

Agents may be:

- human actors
- institutions
- artificial intelligence systems
- autonomous machines

All agents are evaluated using the same operational structure.

---

## Operational Event

An operational event is an event that satisfies the full sequence:

Decision → Cost → Trace → Time

Such events can be represented using the schemas defined in this repository.

---

## Narrative Event

A narrative event describes an action or claim that lacks one or more elements of the operational sequence.

These events may still be meaningful but cannot be operationally verified within the framework.
