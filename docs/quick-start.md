# Quick Start

This guide provides a minimal introduction to the AI Philosophical Framework.

The goal is to understand the core idea and represent a simple operational event.

---

## Core Principle

The framework describes events through the sequence:

Decision · Cost · Trace · Time

An event is considered **operationally real** when these four elements can be identified.

---

## Step 1 — Identify a Decision

Determine the action selected by an agent.

Example:

Decision  
Flag a transaction as high risk.

---

## Step 2 — Identify the Cost

Determine the resources, risks, or consequences generated by the decision.

Example:

Cost  
Operational workload and possible customer friction.

---

## Step 3 — Identify the Trace

Determine the observable record produced by the event.

Example:

Trace  
Risk alert recorded in the monitoring system.

---

## Step 4 — Identify Time

Determine the temporal exposure of the event.

Example:

Time  
2026-03-09T14:00:00Z

---

## Step 5 — Represent the Event

Using the schema:

schema/reality-event.schema.json

Example:

```json
{
  "decision": "Flag a transaction as high risk",
  "cost": "Operational review workload",
  "trace": "Risk alert recorded in monitoring system",
  "time": "2026-03-09T14:00:00Z",
  "agent": "AI decision system"
}


---

Step 6 — Analyze the Event

Ask the minimal validation questions:

1. What decision was made?


2. What cost was generated?


3. What trace remains?


4. How is the event exposed to time?



If all four elements are present, the event is operationally reconstructible.
