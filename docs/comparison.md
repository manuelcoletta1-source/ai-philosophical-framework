# Comparison with Existing Approaches

The AI Philosophical Framework introduces a minimal structure for representing operational events.

The structure is based on the sequence:

Decision · Cost · Trace · Time

This section compares the framework with other approaches used in technological and institutional systems.

---

## Traditional Logging

Traditional logging systems record system activity through logs.

Examples include:

- application logs
- system logs
- database transaction logs

These systems record traces of activity but do not necessarily capture:

- the decision that initiated the event
- the cost generated by the action
- the structural relationship between decision and consequence

The framework explicitly models the decision and cost components.

---

## Audit Trails

Audit trail systems are used to track actions in software or institutional environments.

They typically record:

- who performed an action
- when the action occurred
- what action was performed

Audit trails improve traceability but may not capture the full operational structure of an event.

The framework introduces a broader structure including cost and decision context.

---

## Event Sourcing

Event sourcing is an architectural pattern where system state is derived from a sequence of events.

The AI Philosophical Framework shares similarities with event sourcing in that events are treated as fundamental units of history.

However, the framework focuses on the **operational structure of events**, not only their technical representation.

---

## Explainable AI

Explainable AI aims to make machine learning models more interpretable.

Typical approaches include:

- feature importance
- model explanations
- decision visualization

The framework proposes a complementary perspective: describing AI decisions through reconstructible operational elements.

Decision  
Cost  
Trace  
Time

---

## Philosophical Systems

Traditional philosophical systems often describe human action and responsibility through conceptual or ethical models.

The AI Philosophical Framework differs by introducing a structure intended to be **machine-readable and operationally minimal**.

The goal is not to construct a new philosophical doctrine but to define a minimal structure through which events can be reconstructed across human and artificial systems.

---

## Summary

The framework can be understood as a minimal bridge between:

- philosophical analysis of action
- technological systems that record events
- AI systems that generate decisions

Its purpose is to define a structure through which events remain reconstructible and operationally observable.
