# Framework Glossary

This glossary defines the normative meaning of key terms used in the AI Philosophical Framework.

These definitions apply to schemas, specifications, and validation rules.

---

## Decision

A decision is the selection of one possibility among multiple alternatives by an identifiable agent.

A decision initiates an operational event.

---

## Cost

Cost refers to the resources, risks, sacrifices, or irreversible changes produced by a decision.

Cost indicates that the decision has crossed from possibility into operational reality.

---

## Trace

A trace is an observable record or effect generated by an event.

Traces enable the reconstruction of events.

Examples include logs, records, documents, sensor data, or physical outcomes.

---

## Time

Time refers to the temporal exposure through which an event becomes reconstructible and verifiable.

Temporal exposure allows events to persist, decay, or generate consequences.

---

## Agent

An agent is the entity responsible for initiating a decision.

Agents may include humans, institutions, artificial intelligence systems, or autonomous machines.

---

## Operational Event

An operational event is an event that satisfies the full sequence:

Decision → Cost → Trace → Time

Such events can be represented and validated using the schemas defined in this repository.

---

## Opposable Act

An opposable act is an operational event that can be attributed to a specific agent and contested or verified through available traces.

Opposable acts represent the minimal unit of reconstructible responsibility.

---

## Narrative Event

A narrative event is a description of an action or claim that lacks one or more elements required for operational validation.

Narrative events may exist as interpretation or explanation but cannot be verified through the framework.
